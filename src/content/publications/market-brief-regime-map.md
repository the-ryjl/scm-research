## A map, not a forecast

A regime map organizes what different combinations of growth and inflation might imply for an illustrative asset set. It is not a timing model and does not establish expected returns.

## The four quadrants

- Growth rising, inflation falling
- Growth rising, inflation rising
- Growth falling, inflation falling
- Growth falling, inflation rising

Liquidity and positioning can cause market behavior to differ from the macro label, especially over shorter horizons. For that reason, each quadrant needs both a fundamental description and a market-state overlay.

## What we would monitor

We would track changes rather than levels: revisions to growth expectations, breadth of inflation pressure, credit availability, and cross-asset volatility. The educational value comes from making these inputs explicit before discussing performance.
