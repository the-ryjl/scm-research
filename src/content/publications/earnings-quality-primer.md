## Earnings are a starting point

Reported earnings combine operating performance with judgments about timing, useful lives, reserves, and classification. The goal of an earnings-quality review is not to assume manipulation. It is to understand which assumptions matter and whether cash economics support the reported pattern.

## A repeatable checklist

### Cash conversion

Compare operating cash flow with operating income over multiple periods. Then identify the working-capital accounts responsible for the difference.

### Capitalization

Ask which expenditures are expensed immediately and which become assets. A change in capitalization policy can alter both current earnings and future amortization.

### One-time items

“Adjusted” results can improve comparability, but recurring adjustments deserve special attention. Track categories across time rather than reading each period in isolation.

## Evidence table

| Question | Primary source | Follow-up |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Why did receivables grow? | Cash-flow statement and footnotes | Compare with revenue mix |
| Did useful lives change? | Accounting policy footnote | Recalculate depreciation sensitivity |
| Are adjustments recurring? | Earnings releases | Build a multi-year adjustment log |

## Final note

Accounting analysis is strongest when it produces better operating questions. The statements tell us where to look; they rarely provide the whole explanation by themselves.
