## Four constraints, one reported number

Procedure growth is often discussed as if it were a single demand signal. In practice, reported volume can be constrained by patient willingness, clinician availability, facility capacity, and reimbursement authorization.

## Building a volume bridge

A useful bridge begins with eligible patients and then applies scheduling, approval, and completion rates. Each stage should have a distinct source and an uncertainty range.

| Illustrative driver | Low | Base | High |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Eligible patient growth | 1% | 3% | 5% |
| Capacity improvement | 0% | 2% | 4% |
| Utilization per procedure | -1% | 0% | 1% |

## Channel checks

Potential educational sources include hospital capital budgets, job postings, public reimbursement schedules, clinical trial registries, and interviews conducted under appropriate academic guidelines. No single source should be treated as definitive.

## Risks to the framework

The model can break if clinical practice changes, if reimbursement policy shifts, or if product mix moves faster than volume. Analysts should also distinguish patient backlogs from sustainable underlying demand.
